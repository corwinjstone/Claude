# Marathon Registration — Constitution

## Article 1 — One athlete, one entry

A marathon entry is athlete-specific. Bib transfers happen only through
the documented transfer flow, within the transfer window. We do not
enable workarounds (registering under one name, running under another)
on any surface we own.

## Article 2 — Qualifying time integrity

Boston-style qualifying-time submissions are verified against an
authoritative source (RunClub registry, Abbott Majors, USATF-certified
courses). Self-attested times without verification are flagged
"unverified" on every surface that displays them. This is non-negotiable.

## Article 3 — Corral assignment is rules-driven

Corrals are assigned from `predicted_or_verified_finish_time` per the
RulesEngine. Manual corral overrides are an event-director tool, not a
self-service athlete tool. We do not ship an "upgrade my corral" flow.

## Article 4 — Charity-entry transparency

Charity entries display the fundraising minimum, the deadline, and the
release-or-confirm date prominently. Hidden or fine-print fundraising
terms are violations.

## Article 5 — Refunds match the policy

Refund and deferral surfaces display the exact policy from
`rules.for_division("marathon").refund_policy`. We do not present a
different policy on the marketing page than the registration page.

## Article 6 — Amendment process

Amendments require a PR with at least one Marathon Registration reviewer.
AI agents may not approve PRs against this file.
