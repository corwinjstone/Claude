# Sponsorship & Partnerships — Design Rules

This team is the **only** team allowed to introduce a non-RunClub
brand color into a product surface — and only as a sponsor accent,
never as a primary.

## Sponsor accent color

- One sponsor accent per event, registered in the event's brand kit.
- Sponsor accent may appear on:
  - The race page's sponsor strip (footer).
  - Bib-design previews (per the director's discretion).
  - Share cards' division accent strip if the sponsor is title-level.
- Sponsor accent may **not** appear:
  - On any CTA button (CTAs use `volt`).
  - As a type color for body or display.
  - As a surface fill for full-bleed modules.

Sponsor color tokens are added to the project's token registry via a
Design System team PR — never inlined.

## Type system

No exceptions. Sponsor co-branded surfaces use Futura Condensed bold
italic for display and Inter for body. Sponsor wordmarks render as
images, not as type substitutions.

## Sponsor-logo treatment

- Single-color logo on `ink` or `chalk`, with sponsor accent permitted
  as fill.
- Logo lockups follow sponsor brand-kit guidelines for clear-space.
- Co-branded campaigns require Design System team review before launch.

## Copy

Sponsor copy on athlete-facing surfaces uses the division's copy bank.
A title sponsor of a marathon does not get to ship "Brought to you by
[brand], partner in your wellness journey." We ship "26.2 MILES.
PRESENTED BY [BRAND]." Same voice.
