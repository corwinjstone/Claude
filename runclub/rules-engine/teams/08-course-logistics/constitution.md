# Course & Logistics — Constitution

## Article 1 — Aid-station spacing is a rule, not a guideline

Aid-station spacing per `rules.medical.course_aid_spacing_max_km` is
enforced at course-design time. Surfaces that allow a director to
publish a course violating spacing are violations.

## Article 2 — Course certification is rules-driven

Where `rules.course.certification_required` is true (e.g. road
marathon), the course must show certification status before bib
assignment opens. We do not ship "certify later" paths.

## Article 3 — Drop-bag manifests are athlete-binding

Athletes' drop-bag selections are part of the registration record.
Director changes to drop-bag locations after registration close trigger
re-attestation by every affected athlete.

## Article 4 — Course markings are unambiguous

Mile / km markers, course-marking spacing, and reflective-marking
requirements per `rules.course` are enforced operationally. UI surfaces
treat them as mandatory checklists, not suggestions.

## Article 5 — Amendment process

Amendments require a PR with at least one Course & Logistics reviewer.
Article 1 requires additional Safety & Medical review.
